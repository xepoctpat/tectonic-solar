Space Weather – Earthquake Correlation

Research Background

Some peer-reviewed studies report a statistically significant correlation between geomagnetic storms and seismic activity with a 27–28 day lag — approximately one synodic solar rotation period.

The proposed mechanism involves tidal or electromagnetic stress loading on fault zones during the elevated geomagnetic environment following a CME impact.

Odintsov et al. (2006); Han et al. (2004); Rabeh et al. (2010);
Varga & Grafarend (2018); AGU 2025 poster session.

⚠️ USGS and most seismologists state no proven causal relationship exists. This tool is for research and pattern exploration only — not earthquake prediction.

Active Correlation Window

Any geomagnetic storm (Kp≥5) occurring 27–28 days ago falls within the proposed elevated-risk window for M5+ seismic events.

About This Application

Real-time monitoring of space weather, global seismic activity, and correlation analysis — all in the browser with no backend required.

Live Data Sources

  • USGS Earthquake Catalog — M4.5+ real-time GeoJSON
  • NOAA SWPC — Solar wind, Kp index, X-ray flux
  • Open-Meteo — Weather & air quality (no API key)

Features

  • Live solar wind plasma & Kp index from NOAA DSCOVR/ACE
  • Real Kp 3-day history chart with storm threshold line
  • Real solar wind speed history chart
  • Live USGS earthquake list with depth encoding
  • Magnitude filter slider on map
  • Real weather + air quality via Open-Meteo (free)
  • Browser & in-app toast notifications
  • 27–28 day geomagnetic ↔ seismic correlation timeline
  • Tectonic plate boundaries & motion vectors
  • Settings persistence via localStorage
